About Mark

Mark Bloomfield is a serial entrepreneur and inventor who pioneered technologies and services used by millions of users. From inventing the first computer fax systems to one of the first transportable cell phones, Mark has been on the forefront of technological advancements that truly helped people. His ideas and innovations have resulted in over 20 patents worldwide. With Petitek, Mark hopes to do the same in the pet industry.

As a lifelong animal lover, Mark created ChatrBox from a simple idea to help with his own pets — and quickly saw the magic in it. He transformed that idea into his first tech product for Petitek, with more to come!

Leadership & Innovation

Petitek (2023–Present)
Founder - Innovating the next generation of smart pet technology. Creator of ChatrBox™, a voice-enabled pet trainer with motion detection and behavioral reinforcement features.

HomeASAP (2016–2025)
Foundner - Leading provider of internet marketing and real etate search tools for real estate professionals.

N-Play (2007–2016)
Foundner - Developed social marketing tools on Facebook for the real estate industry. Later rebranded as HomeASAP.

IP Investments Holding, LLC (2006–Present)
Foundner - Focused on building companies from foundational patents and trademarks. Specialized in licensing and monetizing IP portfolios.

AudioFAX Licensing (1996–2006)
Foundner - Managed a portfolio of pioneering patents on computer-based faxing. Successfully licensed the technology to over 50 major telecom and networking companies, including AT&T, Cisco, and Sprint.

AudioFAX Inc. (1986–1996)
Founder - Invented and commercialized fax-store-and-forward technology. Earned over 10 U.S. patents. Sold enhanced fax platforms to five of the seven Bell Operating Companies.

Parrot Communications (1982–1985)
Foudner - Developed one of the first transportable cellular phones in the U.S. Later acquired by General Electric’s Mobile Radio Division.
