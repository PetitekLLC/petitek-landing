About Mark

Mark Bloomfield is a serial entrepreneur and inventor whose pioneering technologies have touched hundreds of millions of users over the last four decades. From inventing the first commercially successful computer-based fax systems to building mobile phones before cell towers existed, Mark has repeatedly been at the forefront of innovation.

Leadership & Innovation

Petitek (2023–Present)
Innovating the next generation of smart pet technology. Creator of ChatrBox™, a voice-enabled pet trainer with motion detection and behavioral reinforcement features.

HomeASAP (2016–Present)
Leading provider of internet marketing tools for real estate professionals. Products include the Real Estate Agent Directory™, HomeASAP.com™, and the Search Alliance™ lead platform.

N-Play (2007–2016)
Developed social marketing tools on Facebook for the real estate industry. Later rebranded as HomeASAP.

IP Investments Holding, LLC (2006–Present)
Focused on building companies from foundational patents and trademarks. Specialized in licensing and monetizing IP portfolios.

AudioFAX Licensing (1996–2006)
Managed a portfolio of pioneering patents on computer-based faxing. Successfully licensed the technology to over 50 major telecom and networking companies, including AT&T, Cisco, and Sprint.

AudioFAX Inc. (1986–1996)
Invented and commercialized fax-store-and-forward technology. Earned over 10 U.S. patents. Sold enhanced fax platforms to five of the seven Bell Operating Companies.

Parrot Communications (1982–1985)
Developed one of the first transportable cellular phones in the U.S. Later acquired by General Electric’s Mobile Radio Division.

Legacy

Mark’s work has resulted in lasting IP value, global product adoption, and multiple successful exits. His technologies have been used by millions — not just in tech labs, but in everyday life.
