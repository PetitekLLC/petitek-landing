Mark Bloomfield

Founder & CEO of Petitek · Technology Pioneer · Visionary Entrepreneur · Animal Lover

About Mark

Mark Bloomfield is a serial entrepreneur and inventor whose technologies have impacted millions of users worldwide. He pioneered several firsts in tech innovation—from the earliest transportable cell phone and the first computer-based fax solution to one of the first real estate applications on Facebook. His work has consistently pushed the boundaries of what’s possible, resulting in more than 20 patents worldwide. With Petitek, Mark is bringing the same spirit of innovation to the pet industry.

As a lifelong animal lover, Mark created ChatrBox from a simple idea to help with his own pets — and quickly saw the magic in it. He transformed that idea into his first tech product for Petitek, with more to come!

Leadership & Innovation

Petitek (2023–Present)
Founder - Innovating the next generation of smart pet technology. Creator of ChatrBox™, a voice-enabled pet trainer with motion detection and behavioral reinforcement features.

HomeASAP (2016–2025)
Founder - Leading provider of internet marketing and real estate search tools for real estate professionals.

N-Play (2007–2016)
Founder - Developed social marketing tools on Facebook for the real estate industry. Later rebranded as HomeASAP.

IP Investments Holding, LLC (2006–Present)
Founder - Focused on building companies from foundational patents and trademarks. Specialized in licensing and monetizing IP portfolios.

AudioFAX Licensing (1996–2006)
Founder - Managed a portfolio of pioneering patents on computer-based faxing. Successfully licensed the technology to over 50 major telecom and networking companies, including AT&T, Cisco, and Sprint.

AudioFAX Inc. (1986–1996)
Founder - Invented and commercialized fax-store-and-forward technology. Earned over 10 U.S. patents. Sold enhanced fax platforms to five of the seven Bell Operating Companies.

Parrot Communications (1982–1985)
Founder - Developed one of the first transportable cellular phones in the U.S. Later acquired by General Electric’s Mobile Radio Division.

Legacy

Mark’s work has resulted in lasting IP value, global product adoption, and multiple successful exits. His technologies have been used by millions — not just in tech labs, but in everyday life.
